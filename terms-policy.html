Terms And Condition

Eligibility

  1. Participants must be registered students of the college hosting the event or associated institutions, as per the event's rules.
  2. Teams must be composed of members from the same college, and team changes after registration are strictly prohibited.

Original Work

  1. All submissions must be original and developed during the hackathon period.
  2. Plagiarism or submission of pre-existing projects will result in immediate disqualification.
  3. Any form of assistance from individuals not part of the registered team is not allowed.

Team composition

  1. Teams must consist of the number of members as specified in the event rules. No changes in team composition will be allowed after registration.
  2. Collaboration within teams is encouraged, but sharing solutions or code between teams is strictly prohibited.

Time Limits

  1. Participants must adhere to the time limits set for each phase of the hackathon, including brainstorming, coding, and presentations.
  2. Late submissions will not be considered unless there is an official extension announced by the organizers.

  1. Participants may use any programming language, tools, and libraries of their choice, as long as they comply with the eventâ€™s guidelines regarding permissible resources.
  2. Proper attribution must be given for any third-party libraries or open-source tools used.

Collaboration and External Assistance

  1. Collaboration within the team is encouraged, but seeking external assistance from individuals or sources outside the team is strictly prohibited.
  2. Violating this rule will lead to disqualification of the team.

Judging Criteria

  1. Creativity and Innovation
  2. Functionality and Usability
  3. Technical Implementation and Feasibility
  4. The decisions of the judges are final and binding. No appeals or objections will be entertained.

Intellectual Property

  2. However, the organizers reserve the right to use submitted projects for promotional purposes without any additional compensation to participants.

  2. Failure to provide adequate documentation may result in lower scores or disqualification.
